DM

David McGlennon

Executive, Leadership & Career Coach

My career started on the shop floor in retail and has taken me through operational management, senior leadership, and Executive Committee roles. Most recently, I served as HR Director for a FTSE 250 European organisation, leading the people strategy across seven countries and supporting business growth, leadership development, and organisational change.

Having progressed from the frontline to board level, I understand the opportunities, challenges, and decisions that shape careers at every stage. For more than 30 years, I've recruited, developed, promoted, and coached people at all levels, from graduates and emerging leaders to senior executives and directors.

What makes me different is simple: I bring experience from both sides of the table. I've supported individuals navigating career transitions and leadership challenges, while also being part of the teams making decisions about recruitment, promotion, succession planning, talent development, and organisational change. That experience allows me to provide coaching that is practical, commercially grounded, and tailored to the realities of today's workplace.

Alongside extensive real-world leadership and coaching experience, I am a Chartered FCIPD professional, an ILM-qualified coach, and an accredited Insights Discovery Practitioner, Belbin Team Role Facilitator, and Leadership Trainer. Together, these qualifications and experiences enable me to help individuals, leaders, and teams build confidence, unlock potential, and achieve meaningful, lasting success.

03 · The Process

Simple, and built
around you.

Coaching is a conversation, not a course. Here is how a typical engagement runs, from the first call to lasting momentum.

01

A free discovery call

We start with a complimentary, no-pressure conversation about where you are now and what you would like to change. If it doesn't feel like the right fit, I will be upfront about that.

02

We agree the focus

Together we set clear goals and decide the right shape: a single session for a specific decision, or a series of sessions for a bigger change.

03

Regular coaching sessions

Practical, confidential one-to-one conversations, at a pace that works around your role and life.

04

Review and momentum

We check progress against what matters to you, adjust where needed, and make sure the change holds well beyond our last conversation.

How much does coaching cost?

Career coaching is £80 per hour and executive coaching is £100 per hour, with discounts for new graduates. Every engagement begins with a free discovery call.

How do sessions work?

We agree a format and pace that suit you, so coaching fits around your role and life.

It depends on your goal. Some people come for a single conversation to work through one decision; others prefer a series. We agree what is right for you at the discovery call.
